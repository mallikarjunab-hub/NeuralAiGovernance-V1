# DSSY (Dayanand Social Security Scheme) - Comprehensive RAG Knowledge Base
## Government of Goa, Department of Social Welfare

## 1. SCHEME OVERVIEW

### Basic Information
- Full Name: Dayanand Social Security Scheme (DSSS/DSSY)
- Notification Number: 50/354/02-03/HC/Part-I/4247
- Notification Date: 2nd October 2002
- Launch Date: 2nd October 2001 (under "Freedom From Hunger" project)
- Effective From: 1st January 2002
- Implementing Department: Directorate of Social Welfare, Government of Goa
- Department Address: 18th June Road, Panjim, Goa
- Department Website: https://socialwelfare.goa.gov.in
- Scheme PDF: https://www.goa.gov.in/wp-content/uploads/2016/05/Dayanand-Social-Security-Scheme.pdf
- Current Beneficiaries: Approximately 1.4 lakh (140,000)
- Annual Financial Liability: Over Rs. 84 crore (as of 2021)

### Objective
To provide monthly financial assistance to the most vulnerable sections of society including:
- Senior Citizens (aged 60 years and above)
- Single Women (widows, divorced, abandoned, judicially separated women, and unmarried women above 50 years)
- Disabled Persons (persons with disabilities)
- HIV/AIDS Patients (Immune Deficiency Persons)

### Historical Context
- Launched as an initiative under "Freedom From Hunger" project on Gandhi Jayanti (2nd October 2001)
- Came into force with effect from 1st January 2002
- Originally provided Rs. 500/- per month with annual increment of Rs. 25
- Scheme has undergone multiple amendments in 2013, 2014, 2016, and 2021

## 2. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

### A. Senior Citizens
- Must be 60 years of age or above at the time of application
- Must be a bonafide resident of the State of Goa by birth or by domicile of 15 years
- Annual family per capita income must be less than the amount of annual financial assistance under the scheme
- Must not be in receipt of financial assistance from any other source (except amalgamated schemes)

### B. Single Women
- Must be above the age of 18 years
- Includes:
  - Widows
  - Divorced women
  - Abandoned women
  - Judicially separated women
  - Unmarried women above the age of 50 years
- If a widow crosses 60 years of age, she can choose financial assistance under either "widow" or "senior citizen" category (not both)

### C. Disabled Persons (Adult)
- Must have valid disability certificate from Medical Board
- Both children and adults with disabilities are covered
- Disability categories include physical disabilities and mental illness

### D. HIV/AIDS Patients
- Immune Deficiency (HIV/AIDS) patients are eligible
- Must provide relevant medical documentation

### E. General Conditions
1. Must be bonafide resident of Goa by birth or domicile of 15 years
2. Must not be in receipt of financial assistance from any other source except schemes amalgamated with DSSS:
   - Dayanand Niradhar Madat Yojana
   - Scheme of Financial Assistance to Young Widows
   - Scheme of Grant of Family Pension to the Old, Destitute, and the Handicapped
3. Annual family per capita income must be less than the annual financial assistance amount
4. After death of beneficiary, spouse is entitled for transfer of financial assistance (must apply in prescribed format)
5. 50% of the financial assistance drawn by the spouse will be given to a maximum of two children each, till they reach age 21, in the event of death of member and spouse (subject to limit of Rs. 1,000/- per child)
6. In case of disabled and HIV positive children, financial assistance will be extended even if parents are availing DSSS or Griha Aadhar
7. Disabled people availing DSSS benefit will continue even after marriage with another disabled DSSS beneficiary, subject to condition of not having gainful employment
8. In case of divorce/legal separation, both husband and wife will be treated as individual members if individually eligible

### Current Rates (Post-2021 Amendment)

|----------|---------------------|

- Senior citizens requiring continuous medication: Rs. 500/- per month
- Requires Medical Certificate from Chief Medical Officer/Health Officer

### Financial Assistance for Aids & Appliances (Persons with Disabilities)
- Maximum up to Rs. 1,00,000 (One Lakh) once in five years
- For aids/appliances as part of enabling environment/rehabilitation
- Requires Medical Certificate from CMO/Health Officer

## 4. APPLICATION PROCEDURE

### Required Documents
1. Birth Certificate- Certificate from Registrar of Births and Deaths, OR age from school records, OR other valid age proof as notified by Government
2. Income Certificate - Family income certificate from competent authority. If unable to obtain, Self Declaration on Rs. 20/- stamp paper in prescribed format, certified and attested before a Gazetted Officer
3. Residence Certificate - 15 years Residence Certificate from Mamlatdar of concerned Taluka. Alternatively, certificate in prescribed format from Gazetted Officer certifying 15 years residency
4. Medical Certificate - In prescribed form from Medical Board (for disabled persons)
5. Death Certificate and Marriage Certificate - For widows. Decree of divorce for divorcees. For unmarried women above 50: self-declaration on Rs. 20/- stamp paper signed by Gazetted Officer
6. Attested copy of Ration Card (Note: As per 2016 amendment, Ration Card requirement withdrawn)
7. Attested copy of Aadhaar Card
8. Attested copy of Election Photo Identity Card
9. Registration Fee - Rs. 200/-. If unable to pay, Rs. 50/- with certificate from MLA/MP

### Application Submission
- Applications submitted to the Director of Social Welfare in the prescribed form
- Applications scrutinized by the Directorate of Social Welfare
- Sanctioned by the Sanctioning Committee

### Sanctioning Committee
- Chief Minister – Chairperson
- Minister for Social Welfare – Member
- Leader of Opposition – Member
- Director of Social Welfare – Member

### Life Certificate Requirement
- Every beneficiary must submit Life Certificate to Director of Social Welfare once a year in April/May
- Certificate can be issued by:
  - Manager of the Bank where monthly financial assistance is deposited, OR
  - Gazetted Officer of the State Government
- Failure to submit = Financial Assistance discontinued

---

## 5. MODE OF PAYMENT

- Financial assistance deposited in Bank Account of beneficiary by Director of Social Welfare every month
- Each beneficiary must open a single account in a Nationalized Bank or Co-operative Bank
- Payment transferred by Electronic Clearance System (ECS)

### Historical Disbursement Channels
- Phase 1 (January 2002): Life Insurance Corporation of India (LIC) - Purchase price model
- Phase 2 (September 2003): Mapusa Urban Co-operative Bank (MUCB)
- Phase 3 (February 2006): Goa State Co-operative Bank (GSCB)
- Current: Direct bank account transfer through ECS

---

## 6. STOPPING/CANCELLATION

Financial Assistance shall be stopped/cancelled if:
1. Beneficiary resorts to professional begging
2. Beneficiary is employed and income exceeds the ceiling income prescribed in the rules

### Interpretation of Provisions
- All interpretation decisions rest with the Government and are final and binding

### Redressal of Grievances
- Addressed to the Secretary to the Government in charge of Science & Technology
- Secretary's decision is final and binding

## 7. SCHEME AMENDMENTS HISTORY

### Original Rules (2001-2002)
- Goa Dayanand Social Security Rules, 2001
- Notification No. 50/354/02-03/HC/Part-I/4247 dated 02/10/2002
- Rs. 500/month with Rs. 25 annual increment

### Amended Scheme (2013)
- Published in Official Gazette dated 22nd September 2014
- Increased to Rs. 2,000/month for general categories
- Rs. 2,500/month for disabled children
- Rs. 3,500/month for 90%+ disability
- Added medical assistance of Rs. 500/month for senior citizens
- Added Rs. 1,00,000 for aids/appliances (PwD category)

### Amendment Rules (2016)
- Published in Official Gazette, Series I No. 2, dated 15th April 2016
- Ration Card requirement withdrawn from application procedure
- Added provisions for disabled and HIV positive children receiving benefits even if parents avail DSSS or Griha Aadhar
- Added provisions for disabled beneficiaries continuing after marriage

### Amendment (2021)
- State Cabinet approved on 29th October 2021
- Increased monthly financial assistance to widows from Rs. 2,000 to Rs. 2,500
- Affected 35,145 widows already availing benefits
- Additional annual financial liability: Over Rs. 21 crore

## 12. CAG AUDIT FINDINGS (2008 Performance Audit)

### Key Issues Identified
1. Off-budget borrowings of Rs. 121.82 crore for pension disbursement without legislative approval
2. Defective beneficiary selection - system lacked accountability, facilitated ineligible persons
3. Non-genuine beneficiaries found in surveys:
   - First survey (January 2004): 11,839 cases (29%) non-genuine out of 40,818
   - Second survey (February 2005): 5,481 cases (25%) non-genuine out of 22,359
4. Failure to stop payments to 12,971 non-genuine beneficiaries = Rs. 43.53 crore avoidable burden
5. Extra penal interest of Rs. 16.91 crore to LIC due to delayed payments
6. Higher service charges to MUCB = Rs. 1.07 crore extra expenditure
7. No books of accounts maintained by the department
8. Improper processing - income criteria not verified against ration card declarations

### CAG Recommendations
1. Assess eligibility of all beneficiaries de novo and weed out ineligible ones
2. Take immediate action to recover financial assistance given to non-genuine beneficiaries
3. Establish mechanism to maintain complete records of beneficiaries

## 15. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQs)

Q: What is DSSY/DSSS?
A: Dayanand Social Security Scheme is a monthly pension scheme by the Government of Goa providing financial assistance to senior citizens (60+), single women, disabled persons, and HIV/AIDS patients.

Q: How much pension do senior citizens receive?
A: Rs. 2,000 per month. Additionally, those needing continuous medication can avail medical assistance of Rs. 500 per month.

Q: How much do widows receive under DSSY?
A: Rs. 2,500 per month (increased from Rs. 2,000 in October 2021).

Q: What is the pension for disabled persons?
A: Rs. 2,000/month for adult disabled; Rs. 2,500/month for disabled children (below 90% disability); Rs. 3,500/month for persons with 90% and above disability.

Q: Who is eligible for DSSY?
A: Goa residents (by birth or 15 years domicile) who are senior citizens (60+), single women (18+), disabled persons, or HIV/AIDS patients, with family per capita income less than the annual assistance amount.

Q: How to apply for DSSY?
A: Submit application with required documents to the Director of Social Welfare. Registration fee is Rs. 200 (or Rs. 50 with MLA/MP certificate).

Q: Is there a residency requirement?
A: Yes, the applicant must be a bonafide resident of Goa by birth or by domicile of 15 years.

Q: Can both husband and wife receive DSSY?
A: Only one spouse can receive DSSY at a time. After death of beneficiary, the other spouse can apply for transfer.

Q: What is the Life Certificate requirement?
A: Every beneficiary must submit a Life Certificate annually in April/May, issued by the bank manager or a Gazetted Officer.

Q: When was DSSY launched?
A: On 2nd October 2001, effective from 1st January 2002.

Q: How many people benefit from DSSY?
A: Approximately 1.4 lakh (140,000) beneficiaries as of recent data.

Q: What happens if I don't submit the Life Certificate?
A: Financial assistance will be discontinued.

Q: Can I avail DSSY with Griha Aadhar scheme?
A: Generally no, but disabled and HIV positive children can receive DSSY even if parents avail Griha Aadhar.

Q: What is the difference between DSSY and DDSSY?
A: DSSY (Dayanand Social Security Scheme) is a pension/financial assistance scheme. DDSSY (Deen Dayal Swasthya Seva Yojana) is a separate health insurance scheme launched on 30 May 2016.
